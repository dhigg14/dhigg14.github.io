Review:

(Spoilers duh)

I love to play video games and I played alot of them, But I would like to prove that by analyzing one game. This game I played alot as a kid and looking back at it, it didn't really hold up. The game I am talking about is Pac-Man World 3 (2005) and yeah I loved this game as a kid and it did not age well. This game is a sequel to my favorite Pac-Man World game, Pac-Man World 2 (2002), but that game was more of a mario bros level to level type game where the level design was more linear than in World 3. World 3 still had levels but they were more open-world type then linear. But other than level design I would like to go over 3 things of the game: Story, Gameplay, and Sound design.

Story

So the story of Pac-Man World 3 is that it's Pac-Man's 25th birthday because this game came out during Pac-Man's 25th anniversary and he was suddenly teleported away from his family from a ghost name Orson who was the final boss of the first Pac-Man World game. Orson basically told Pac-Man that Inky, Blinky, Pinky and Clyde the 4 ghost from all the Pac-Man games including the first arcade of Pac-Man were kidnap by some guy name Erwin and he wants to use the power of the ghost world a.k.a the Spectral Realm to destroy Pac-Land, Pac-Man Hometown. Now the story sounds interesting but I have a few problems with it, how is Orson now a good guy after being eaten by Pac-Man at the end of World 1, the game really never really gave us an answer we just have to accept it. Also what kind of bad guy name is Erwin, sounds like an executive at some computer company and why does he want to destory Pac-Land well the Game never explain and we just have to defeat the big bad guy. It's like the game just needed a bad guy because they forgot to put one. Also I find it ridiculous that we went from Pac-Man eating ghost and saving the day from ghost to some random scientist wanting the ghost energy to destory main character home and it's kind of dumb. So yeah the story is ok but with flaws but that's not the reason this game is bad, no no it's because of the Gameplay.
